What are the Power BI Desktop Features? 12

a.    Connect to hundreds of on-premises and cloud-based data sources; This lets you explore your data wherever it is—in the cloud or on-premises—including big data sources such as Hadoop, SalesForce, Excel and Spark. With connections to hundreds of sources and constantly growing, Power BI Desktop lets you draw deep insights for a broad range of scenarios

b.    Clean and prepare data using visual tools; This lets you prepare and model your data with ease using Power Query. Familiarity with Excel reporting benefits the Power BI user.  With Power BI Desktop data modeling you can clean, transform, and mash up data from multiple sources—in a few clicks. Reclaim hours in your day.

c.    Analyze and build stunning reports with custom visualizations; Power BI desktop provides advanced analytics with the familiarity of excel. It enables business users to dig deeper into data and find patterns they may have otherwise missed, with Power BI features like quick measures, grouping, forecasting, and clustering. Advanced users have full control over their model using powerful DAX formula language. If you’re familiar with Excel, you’ll feel at home in Power BI.

d.    Publish to the Power BI service; Create Interactive reports customized for your business. Power BI Desktop let you create stunning reports with interactive data visualizations. Tell your data story using a drag-and-drop canvas and more than 85 modern data visuals from Microsoft and partners—or create your own using the Power BI open source custom visuals framework. Design your report using theming, formatting, and layout tools.

e.    Embed in public websites; Author for anyone everyone, anywhere. With Power BI desktop you can get visual analytics to the people who need it. Create mobile-optimized reports for viewers to consume on the go. Publish from Power BI Desktop to the cloud or on-premises. Embed reports created in Power BI Desktop into existing apps or websites.
